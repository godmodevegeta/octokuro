# Socrates Trademark Policy

The Socrates name, stylized wordmark, logo, owl mark, and related visual identity are referred to here as the "Socrates Marks." They are marks claimed by the Socrates project owner. This policy does not claim that any mark is registered.

The `AGPL-3.0-only` software license does not grant permission to use the Socrates Marks as branding. This trademark policy is separate from the copyright license and does not limit truthful references to the project.

## Uses that do not require permission

You may:

- refer to Socrates accurately in articles, tutorials, reviews, and compatibility statements;
- link to the canonical Socrates repository or website;
- show unaltered screenshots for commentary, education, or reporting; and
- say that another project is "based on Socrates" or "compatible with Socrates" when that statement is accurate and does not imply endorsement.

## Uses that require permission

You need prior written permission to:

- use a Socrates Mark in the name of a product, service, company, domain, package, or social account;
- use the Socrates logo, or a confusingly similar variation, as another project's identity;
- imply that a modified build, hosted service, or commercial offering is official, sponsored, or endorsed by Socrates; or
- use a Socrates Mark on merchandise or in paid advertising as your own brand.

## Forks and redistributed builds

Publicly distributed modified versions must use a distinct product name and visual identity and must not present themselves as an official Socrates release. They may state factually that they are based on Socrates. Copyright notices, license notices, and source-code obligations remain governed by the AGPL.

An unmodified redistribution may identify the software as Socrates, but it must not describe the distributor or build as official unless the project owner has authorized that claim.

For trademark permission, use the contact method published by the owner of the canonical Socrates repository.
